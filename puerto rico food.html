Pasteles Recipe

Pasteles are similar to tamales, but they are boiled in banana leaves instead of steamed in corn husks, and they are filled with a root vegetable or banana base instead of finely-ground corn flour.

Ingredients

  • Green bananas (guineos): Grated and processed into a paste for flavor and texture in the dough.
  • Plantains: Used to make the masa.
  • Yautia or yuca: Also used to make the masa.
  • Achiote oil: Used to season the masa.
  • Pork, ham, or chicken: Used for the filling.
  • Sofrito: A mixture of peppers and onions used to simmer the filling.
  • Garbanzos, olives, capers, and raisins: Mixed into the filling.
  • Banana leaves: Used to wrap the pasteles.

Instructions

To cook pasteles:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the pasteles in a single layer.
  3. Cook for about an hour. If the pasteles are frozen, they may take an additional 10 minutes to cook.
  4. After cooking, unwrap the pasteles and serve them immediately.
